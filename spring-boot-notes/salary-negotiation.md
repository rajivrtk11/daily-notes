---

# 🔹 1. Introduction / Tell me about yourself

**HR:** “Tell me about yourself”

**You (keep it ~60–90 sec):**

> “I’m currently working as a backend developer with around X years of experience, primarily in Java, Spring Boot, and microservices.
> In my current role, I’ve been responsible for designing and developing scalable services, optimizing performance, and handling production issues.
> I’ve also started taking ownership of modules and mentoring junior team members, which naturally aligns with a lead role.
> Now I’m looking to move into a position where I can contribute more from a leadership and system design perspective.”

---

# 🔹 2. Current Role Deep Dive

**HR:** “What exactly are you doing in your current role?”

**You:**

> “I work on building and maintaining microservices using Spring Boot. My responsibilities include API design, performance optimization, and handling production issues.
> I also collaborate with cross-functional teams and help junior developers with code reviews and design discussions.”

---

# 🔹 3. Why are you looking for a change?

**HR:** “Why do you want to leave your current company?”

**You:**

> “I’ve learned a lot in my current role, but now I’m looking for more ownership and a clear transition into a lead position, where I can contribute not just technically but also in guiding the team and making architectural decisions.”

---

# 🔹 4. Why Capgemini?

**HR:** “Why do you want to join Capgemini?”

**You:**

> “Capgemini has strong exposure to large-scale enterprise systems and diverse projects. I’m particularly interested in working in an environment where I can contribute to complex systems and grow into a leadership role while working with distributed teams.”

---

# 🔹 5. Leadership Check

**HR:** “Have you handled a team?”

**You (don’t panic if informal):**

> “While I may not have had an official title, I’ve taken ownership of modules and mentored junior developers. I’ve been involved in code reviews, resolving blockers, and ensuring timely delivery, which has given me hands-on experience in team coordination.”

---

# 🔹 6. Salary Discussion Starts

**HR:** “What is your current CTC?”

**You:**

> “My current CTC is 13 LPA.”

---

# 🔹 7. Expected Salary

**HR:** “What are your expectations?”

**You (confident, no hesitation):**

> “I’m looking at a range of **25–30 LPA**, considering my experience, skill set, and the expectations of this role.”

👉 Then **STOP talking**

---

# 🔹 8. Justification Question (CRITICAL)

**HR:** “That’s a big jump. Can you justify this?”

**You:**

> “I’ve based my expectation on market standards for a Java Lead role and my experience working on microservices and scalable systems.
> I’ve also taken ownership and mentored team members, which aligns with lead responsibilities.
> So I’m aligning my expectation with both the role scope and the value I can bring.”

---

# 🔹 9. Pushback Scenario (very likely)

**HR:** “We may not be able to go that high. Our budget is around 20–22.”

**You (don’t fold):**

> “I understand budget constraints. However, considering this is a lead role and the expectations involved, I’d be more comfortable closer to the mid to higher end of my range.
> Is there flexibility on the budget?”

---

# 🔹 10. If they give an offer (say ₹22–24)

**HR:** “We can offer 23 LPA.”

**You (important moment):**

> “That’s close to my expectation. I’m really interested in the role.
> Is there any possibility to move closer to **25 LPA**?”

👉 This one line can give you +2L

---

# 🔹 11. Notice Period

**HR:** “What is your notice period?”

**You:**

> “My notice period is X days. However, I can try to negotiate or reduce it if required.”

---

# 🔹 12. Closing Question

**HR:** “Do you have any questions for us?”

**You (ALWAYS ask):**

> “Yes, I’d like to understand:
>
> * What would be the immediate expectations from this role in the first 3–6 months?
> * How is success measured for this position?”

---

# 🔥 Final Impression Line (very powerful)

Before ending, say:

> “I’m really interested in this opportunity and the role. If we can align on the compensation, I’d be happy to move forward.”

---

# ❗ Golden Rules (don’t forget)

* Speak **calmly, not defensively**
* Don’t rush to accept
* Don’t justify using personal reasons
* Always **tie salary to role + value**
